TrumpRx vs GoodRx vs LillyDirect: Which Saves You the Most?

Updated April 20, 2026

Three platforms dominate GLP-1 self-pay pricing in 2026, and they're built on completely different business models. TrumpRx is a federal self-pay program with negotiated flat rates. GoodRx is a coupon aggregator pulling from third-party discount networks. LillyDirect is Eli Lilly's direct-to-consumer channel for their own drugs.

None of them wins for every drug. The right answer depends on which GLP-1 you need. This is a head-to-head breakdown.

How the Three Platforms Work

TrumpRx

Federal self-pay program launched in 2025 as part of drug pricing transparency efforts. Negotiates flat monthly rates directly with manufacturers, so prices stay stable across doses and pharmacies. Works for uninsured patients, Medicare beneficiaries (who can use it as a Part D alternative when their plan doesn't cover a drug), and anyone else paying cash. Pharmacy availability varies by location.

GoodRx

Coupon aggregator. Doesn't negotiate directly with manufacturers; instead pulls from Pharmacy Benefit Manager (PBM) discount networks. Prices fluctuate based on what PBMs publish, which means quoted prices can change week-to-week. Widely accepted at chain pharmacies. Often has introductory or promotional pricing on specific drugs.

LillyDirect

Eli Lilly's direct-to-consumer pharmacy. Only sells Lilly drugs: currently Zepbound and Foundayo (Mounjaro was removed in April 2026). Offers tiered pricing by dose. Ships directly to your home. Does not bill insurance at all — always self-pay.

When Each Platform Wins Overall

TrumpRx wins when:

  • You're on a maintenance dose (flat pricing beats every tiered alternative).
  • You need Mounjaro (nothing else is competitive).
  • You need Wegovy long-term (NovoCare and GoodRx both get expensive after initial period).
  • You need consistent pricing across monthly refills (no week-to-week fluctuation).

GoodRx wins when:

  • You need a short-term bridge (intro pricing often matches TrumpRx for 1–2 fills).
  • You're on lower doses of semaglutide drugs (starter-dose Ozempic, intro Wegovy injection).
  • Your local pharmacy doesn't accept TrumpRx but does take GoodRx coupons.

LillyDirect wins when:

  • You need Foundayo (TrumpRx doesn't carry it yet).
  • You're on the Zepbound 2.5mg starter dose and prefer direct-from-manufacturer shipping.
  • You don't have access to a TrumpRx-participating pharmacy.

Caveats

  • TrumpRx pharmacy availability: Not every pharmacy accepts TrumpRx pricing. Call ahead or use the TrumpRx pharmacy locator.
  • GoodRx prices fluctuate: Quoted prices can change week-to-week. Always verify at checkout.
  • LillyDirect requires Lilly's own pharmacy: You can't use your local pharmacist. The medication ships to your home.
  • None of these stack with savings cards: If you have commercial insurance that covers your GLP-1, the savings card ($25/month) beats every discount platform listed here.

Overall Winner: TrumpRx

If we had to pick one platform for most GLP-1 shoppers in 2026, it's TrumpRx. Flat pricing, broad coverage across semaglutide and tirzepatide drugs, and consistent wins at maintenance doses. GoodRx remains useful for short-term or starter-dose situations; LillyDirect remains the go-to for Foundayo and for Zepbound starter users who want direct shipping.
